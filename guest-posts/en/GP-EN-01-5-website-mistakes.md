## Mistake #1: Your site loads too slowly

Speed is a Google ranking factor. Since 2021, Core Web Vitals — page load speed, visual stability and interactivity — are built directly into Google's ranking algorithm. A site scoring below 70 on PageSpeed Insights is being actively penalised.

The business impact: 53% of mobile visitors abandon a page taking more than 3 seconds to load. A 1-second delay reduces conversions by 7%.

**The fix:** Edge-deployed stack. WebP images with explicit dimensions. No render-blocking scripts. CDN. Most sites go from a score of 40 to 95+ with the right technical approach.

---

## Mistake #2: Your homepage doesn't answer the three critical questions

A first-time visitor asks three things within 5 seconds:
1. What does this business do?
2. Is it relevant to me?
3. Why should I trust them?

"Welcome" answers none of them. Neither does "Innovative solutions for modern challenges."

❌ "Creative agency delivering digital excellence"
✅ "We build websites for professional services firms that need to win more clients online"

The second is specific. It immediately qualifies the visitor and tells them whether they're in the right place.

---

## Mistake #3: No trust signals above the fold

Buying decisions are trust decisions. Before contacting you, a prospect needs evidence others like them have hired you and been satisfied.

Most businesses bury testimonials on a separate page nobody visits. Put your strongest trust signal in the first screenful of the homepage — one real testimonial with a name and role beats an entire "testimonials page".

---

## Mistake #4: Weak or buried call to action

Every page needs one clear primary action. Most sites have either no clear CTA, one buried too far down, or five competing CTAs creating paralysis.

❌ "Contact us" / "Learn more"
✅ "Get a free website audit" / "Start your project today"

Action language with a clear value proposition outperforms passive language by 2–4× in click-through rate.

---

## Mistake #5: Broken SEO foundations

Roughly 85% of business websites have critical preventable SEO problems:

- Title tags that are just the company name (not keyword-optimised)
- No schema.org structured data (missing FAQ rich results, service listings)
- Sitemap not submitted to Google Search Console
- Duplicate content with no canonical tags

These aren't advanced tactics — they're basic hygiene. Not having them is like having a great shopfront with no sign above the door.

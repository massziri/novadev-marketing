## What a website audit covers

**1. Technical performance**
Speed, Core Web Vitals, mobile rendering. Affects both user experience and Google rankings.
*Typical issues:* oversized images, render-blocking scripts, slow server response, no CDN.

**2. SEO architecture**
Title tags, meta descriptions, heading hierarchy, schema.org data, sitemap, canonical tags.
*Typical issues:* duplicate title tags, no schema markup, no sitemap in Search Console.

**3. Design and user experience**
Clear value proposition, visual hierarchy, mobile experience, trust signals placement.
*Typical issues:* unclear homepage message, trust signals buried below the fold.

**4. Conversion optimisation**
CTAs, contact friction, pricing transparency, social proof placement.
*Typical issues:* weak CTAs, contact form too far down, no testimonials visible.

## What audit findings typically look like

Across hundreds of business websites audited:

- **Performance issues** (~90%): Mobile PageSpeed below 65, images 3–10× too large, no CDN
- **SEO issues** (~85%): Title tags = just the company name, no schema.org, sitemap not submitted
- **Design/UX issues** (~70%): Homepage doesn't answer what + for whom + why, no above-fold testimonials
- **Conversion issues** (~80%): CTA buried, contact form 4+ scrolls down, no starting price visible
